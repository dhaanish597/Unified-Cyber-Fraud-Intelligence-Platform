Your contribution — the fusion overlay

No public dataset links a user's login/cyber events to their fraud transactions. That gap is the market gap. So you synthesize the link — and this becomes your defensible, reproducible innovation:

  • Take the transaction backbone (PaySim / IEEE-CIS) and assign each row a synthetic user_id, device, and IP.
  • Generate a per-user cyber event log: mostly normal logins, plus injected compromise sequences drawn from UNSW-NB15 patterns — impossible-travel login, new-device MFA-cookie reuse, credential-stuffing success.
  • For a chosen slice of frauds, place a cyber-compromise event 30–120 s before the fraudulent transfer. This is the correlated signal.
  • Define a fused label: a transaction is high-risk if a compromise event for that user fired inside the pre-window. Now your engine can learn and prove the correlation.
  • The headline metric becomes the fusion uplift: recall on "cyber-preceded" frauds for the tabular-only baseline vs. the fused engine. The delta is your story.

Frame it honestly to judges as a controlled evaluation harness: you're not claiming a real bank's joined data; you're demonstrating that when the correlation exists, fusion recovers frauds the silos miss. That is exactly the point a bank CISO wants proven.

08

Judge Q&A — the four they'll press

Your dossier's Section 27 covers the soft questions. These are the hard ones an industry panel will actually ask. Have the answers reflexive.

1 · "You joined cyber and fraud data — but that joined dataset doesn't exist publicly. What did you actually train on?"

"Correct, and that absence is the market gap. We built a controlled evaluation harness: a real transaction backbone (PaySim/IEEE-CIS) with a synthetic cyber-event overlay where compromise events precede a labelled slice of frauds. It lets us prove that when the correlation exists, fusion recovers frauds the silos miss. In production this signal comes from the bank's own SIEM + CBS feeds — exactly what a pilot would wire up."

2 · "What's your PR-AUC and false-positive rate at your operating threshold — not accuracy?"

"On a 0.129%-fraud set, accuracy is meaningless — a do-nothing model scores 99.8%. We report PR-AUC, F1, and recall at a fixed low FPR, plus the confusion matrix. Here are the real numbers." (Have the metrics report open.)

3 · "What's genuinely built versus slideware?"

"Everything in the live demo runs: the fusion engine, the trained models, the graph, the dashboard, the XAI, the quantum monitor, the CERT-In report. Kafka, Flink, Kubernetes and federated learning are our production architecture — on the roadmap, not faked in the prototype. We'd rather show you a real slice than a fake whole."

4 · "GraphSAGE in a 50–100 ms payment-authorization window — how?"

"Tiered scoring. LightGBM scores inline in under 10 ms and is never blocked. Graph embeddings are precomputed and refreshed asynchronously — GraphSAGE is inductive, so new accounts get embeddings without retraining the whole graph. The deep traversal informs the score; it doesn't sit in the critical path."
